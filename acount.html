Accounting & Taxation

Understand your tax obligations and choose the right ITR form.

Income Tax Return (ITR)

ITR is filed by taxpayers to report income and tax liability.

  • ITR-1: For salaried individuals and pensioners with income up to ₹50 lakhs.
  • ITR-2: For individuals and HUFs with capital gains or business income.
  • ITR-3: For individuals and HUFs earning from business or profession.
  • ITR-4: Simplified form for self-employed professionals (income up to ₹50 lakhs).
  • ITR-5: For businesses, partnerships, and LLPs.
  • ITR-6: For companies not exempt under Section 11.
  • ITR-7: For trusts, political parties, and educational institutions.
  • ITR-U: For revising incorrect filings or disclosing undisclosed income.

Correction of ITR

Mistakes in ITR can be corrected by filing a revised return within one year from the assessment year.

Response to Notice

If you receive a notice from the Income Tax Department, you must respond within the specified time to avoid penalties.

Which ITR Form Should You File?

The correct form depends on your income and tax status. Consult your SRM if unsure.
